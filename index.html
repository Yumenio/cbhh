We provide the following customized personal pediatric services to each of our patients
  • VAC Machine Therapy: Vacuum-assisted closure of a wound is a type of therapy to help wounds heal. During the treatment, a device decreases air pressure on the wound. This helps the wound heal more quickly.

  • Subcutaneous Injections: Subcutaneous means under the skin. In this type of injection, a short needle is used to inject a drug into the tissue layer between the skin and the muscle.

  • Decubitus Ulcer Care: A Decubitus Ulcer, also called a pressure sore or bed sore, is an open wound on your skin. Pressure sores often occur on the skin covering bony areas. The condition is highly treatable and recovery is good with proper diagnosis and treatment.

  • Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy is a drug treatment that uses powerful chemicals to kill fast-growing cells in your body. Chemotherapy is most often used to treat cancer, since cancer cells grow and multiply much more quickly than most cells in the body.

  • Compliance Monitoring: Compliance Monitoring or Medication Management is the process by which Clinicians closely monitors a patient to ensure they are taking their medications correctly, and following their prescription regimen to help achieve desired/favorable outcomes.

  • IV Therapy: Intravenous therapy (abbreviated as IV therapy) is a medical technique that delivers a liquid directly into a person's vein. The intravenous route of administration is commonly used for rehydration solutions or to provide nutrition in those who cannot consume food or water by mouth. It may also be used to administer medications or other medical therapy such as blood products or electrolytes to correct electrolyte imbalances.

  • Wound Care: Wound care is important for wound healing. Wound care can prevent more serious complications from occuring. Wound dressings, collagen-specific enzymes, sulfa antibiotics, and antiseptics are used to prevent infection and to speed healing.

  • Central Line Care: A central line is an IV (intravenous) line that goes into a large blood vessel near the center of the body. Central lines are used for giving medications, fluids, IV nutrition and drawing blood.
